# How to give feedback / input

The plan here is to produce modular chunks that are open to iterative feedback, whether that's from me directly pestering people or others actually cloning the repo and doing pull requests.

The goal is to do open, test/learn-friendly research that can be useful/accessible/feedback-able across institutional boundaries.

I want to try and make it so feedback can come from any direction and take any form, so anyone in any role can make comments/suggestions/criticisms small or large. 

If you're happy with your name being attached to those, I'll do that, but I'll assume without express permission that's not the case.

## Where Feedback Lives

I'll aim to put all feedback into the **GitHub Issues** on this repository. This creates a single, public, timestamped record of the conversation around the work and acts as a place I can keep track of my responses.

Browse existing feedback: [Issues page](../../issues)

## You Don't Need to Use GitHub

If you'd prefer to get in touch directly — by **email** or **LinkedIn** (see below) or any other route — that's completely fine.

Saying again:

- I'll summarise your feedback as a GitHub Issue so it becomes part of the project record.
- **I will only do this with your express permission.** I'll check with you first.
- If you'd prefer your feedback to be **anonymous or unattributed**, just say so.
- If you'd rather your feedback stayed **entirely private**, that's fine too.

## If You Want to Use GitHub Directly and you're new to it...

1. **Create a GitHub account** (free, ~2 minutes): [github.com/signup](https://github.com/signup)
2. **Go to the Issues page**: [Issues](../../issues)
3. **Click "New Issue"** and choose a template
4. **Write your feedback** and click "Submit"

That's it. I'll be notified and will respond.

## A Note for Public Sector Colleagues

I understand that commenting publicly may not always be straightforward. You can email me directly, and I'll check before logging anything publicly. You can be anonymous or unattributed — just let me know.
